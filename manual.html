User Guide

A step by step walkthrough of your food truck day from first setup to close. Jump to any section using the sidebar.

1

Setting Up Your Menu

Do this before your first day of service. Your menu syncs instantly across all devices so every station sees the same items and prices. Start with categories, then fill in your items.

  • 1Open Menu from the sidebar. Tap Add Item in the top right corner.
  • 2Enter the Item Name, Category, Price, and Prep Time in minutes. Default categories include Mains, Sides, Drinks, Specials, Desserts, and Snacks.
  • 3Toggle Available for ordering on. Items toggled off will not appear on the order screen until you switch them back on.
  • 4Tap Save. Repeat for each item on your menu.
  • 5To add a custom category directly while creating an item, tap the + chip at the end of the category row. Type your new category name in the prompt and tap Add. You can also tap Categories in the menu header to manage all categories at once, rename them, or delete empty ones.
Tip: Use the availability toggle each morning to hide items you are out of. They stay saved and turn back on whenever you need them.
2

Starting Your Day

When the app opens and the day has not been started, a Start of Day screen appears automatically. It asks who is starting the shift so the right flow opens: the owner counts the opening float, employees clock in. The sidebar tracks the session status all day.

  • 1Open the app. The Start of Day screen appears automatically if the till has not been opened yet. Choose I'm the Owner to count your opening float, or I'm an Employee to clock in for your shift.
  • 2On the Opening Float screen, tap + next to each denomination to count your bills and coins. The total calculates automatically as you tap.
  • 3Tap Start Day with $XX.XX. The sidebar card switches from the orange Day Not Started state to a green Day Active banner showing your float.
  • 4If an employee is starting, they tap I'm an Employee and select their name from the staff roster to clock in. The float count is not required for employee clock-in.
  • 5If you tap Skip, the orange Day Not Started card stays in the sidebar. Tap Start Day there anytime, or from the orange banner that appears at the top of the Orders screen.
Tip: Set a business opening time in Settings under Start Time. The Start of Day screen will only appear automatically at or after that time, which is useful for trucks with fixed opening hours.
3

Taking a Customer Order

The order screen is split into two panels: the menu grid on the left and the cart on the right. Tap items to add them, adjust quantities inline, then send the order to the kitchen with one tap.

  • 1When a customer walks up, optionally type their name and phone number in the Customer field at the top of the cart. This enables SMS notifications on Enterprise.
  • 2Tap items in the menu grid to add them to the cart. Use + / - to adjust quantities.
  • 3Tap Charge $X.XX to send the order to the kitchen and proceed to payment.
Tip: Taking the customer name is optional but recommended. On Enterprise, any customer with a phone number on their order automatically gets a text when their food is ready.
4

Kitchen Display

The kitchen screen runs on a separate device and updates in real time as orders are placed. Your cook sees each ticket the moment the cashier taps Charge. Tap Mark Ready when food is plated.

  • 1Open the app on your kitchen device and tap Kitchen Display in the sidebar. The status badge shows the live connection.
  • 2Pending orders appear on the left. Each ticket shows the order number, elapsed time, customer name, and item list.
  • 3Tap Mark Ready ✓ when the order is plated. It moves to the Ready column. If the customer has a phone number, they get an SMS automatically on Enterprise.
Tip: The timer color changes from green to yellow to red as minutes pass. Red means an order has been waiting over 10 minutes.
5

Taking Payment

After tapping Charge, the payment sheet slides up. Choose cash, card, external terminal, or Square. Cash automatically calculates change due. Optionally add a tip or discount before confirming.

  • 1Tap the payment method: Cash, Card, External Terminal, or Square.
  • 2For cash, enter the amount tendered. Change due is calculated instantly.
  • 3Select a tip percentage or enter a custom amount. Apply a discount if needed. Tap Confirm Payment.
Tip: Select External Terminal or Square when your card reader handles the charge separately. The app records the transaction without processing payment itself.
6

Customer Notifications via SMS

ENTERPRISE
🔒 This feature requires the Enterprise plan. View pricing to upgrade.

Connect your own Twilio account once in Settings and your truck automatically texts customers when their order is ready. You pay Twilio directly at their rates with no markup from us.

  • 1Go to Settings → SMS Notifications. Log in to twilio.com and copy your Account SID and Auth Token from the console dashboard.
  • 2Paste both values into the fields and tap Connect Twilio. Your Twilio phone number is detected automatically.
  • 3From now on, any customer who has a phone number on their order will get a text from your truck name when the kitchen marks the order ready. No phone number? It skips silently.
Tip: You only need to connect Twilio once. It stays connected until you explicitly disconnect. No extra configuration is needed beyond pasting your credentials.
7

Tracking Customers

PRO
🔒 This feature requires the Pro plan. View pricing to upgrade. All your order data is already stored and will appear instantly once you upgrade.

Customer profiles are created automatically when a name is entered at order time. Use the Customers screen to look up returning customers, see their visit history, and note any allergy information.

  • 1Open Customers from the sidebar. Search by name, phone, or email in the search bar.
  • 2Tap a customer to view their contact details, total visits, lifetime spend, and full order history.
  • 3Any allergy notes you add show as a red alert on the customer row so kitchen staff are always reminded.
8

Daily Reports

PRO
🔒 This feature requires the Pro plan. View pricing to upgrade.

The Reports screen shows a live summary of your revenue, orders, and top items. Switch between Today, This Week, and This Month to see trends. An end of day email report is sent to your inbox automatically when you close your shift.

  • 1Open Reports. Tap Today, This Week, or This Month to change the period.
  • 2Review Gross Revenue, Tax Collected, Total Orders, and the payment breakdown by cash vs. card.
  • 3Scroll to Top Selling Items to see which items are driving the most volume and revenue.
9

Managing Inventory

PRO
🔒 This feature requires the Pro plan. View pricing to upgrade.

Track your ingredients, set minimum stock thresholds, and link each ingredient to menu items to calculate your cost of goods automatically. The app flags anything below your minimum so you reorder before you run out.

  • 1Open Inventory. On the Ingredients tab, tap + Add Ingredient. Enter the name, unit (kg, L, pcs), cost per unit, current stock, and minimum stock.
  • 2Switch to the Recipe Costs tab to link ingredients to menu items. The app calculates your cost per item and margin automatically.
  • 3Use the Variance tab to compare expected versus actual stock and find where product is being lost.
10

Logging Expenses

PRO
🔒 This feature requires the Pro plan. View pricing to upgrade.

Log any business cost here as it happens: fuel, permits, supplies, equipment repairs. Every expense feeds directly into your Profit and Loss report so your net profit number is always accurate.

  • 1Open Expenses. Tap + Add Expense.
  • 2Enter the Amount, pick a Category (Fuel, Permits, Equipment, Supplies, and more), and write a brief Description.
  • 3Tap Add Expense. The total updates immediately and rolls into your P&L automatically.
11

Profit and Loss

PRO
🔒 This feature requires the Pro plan. View pricing to upgrade.

The P&L screen pulls together your revenue, cost of goods, labor, and expenses into one statement. Read it at the end of each month to understand whether your truck is profitable and where costs are eating into your margin.

  • 1Open Profit and Loss. Select This Month or Last 30 Days for a meaningful view.
  • 2Read the Net Profit card. Green means profitable, red means costs exceeded revenue for that period.
  • 3Drill into the Statement section to see exactly which cost category is largest: goods, labor, or expenses.
12

Closing Your Day

When service is over, close the session from the sidebar. The till resets overnight so the next app launch prompts you to start a fresh day. On Pro and Enterprise an end of day email report is sent to your inbox automatically.

  • 1Tap End Day on the green Day Active card in the sidebar. You can also reach this from Settings → Day Controls → End of Day Report.
  • 2The end of day screen shows today's total orders, gross revenue, and payment breakdown.
  • 3Count your closing cash drawer by denomination. The app compares your count to the expected total (opening float plus cash sales) and shows any overage or shortage.
  • 4Tap Close Day to finalize. On Pro and Enterprise, a summary report is emailed to your account address automatically. The next app launch will prompt you to start the day again.
Tip: Closing the day resets the session so the next app launch triggers the Start of Day screen automatically. If the variance in your closing count is consistently off, check whether all cash payments were recorded correctly.
13

Staff and PIN Lock

The owner account signed in on the iPad always has full access — there is no separate device PIN to unlock the app. Staff clock in with their own 4-digit PIN only when they want to switch into their restricted role, so the owner is never locked out of their own iPad.

  • 1Each employee is given a 4-digit PIN when you add them under Labor. Assign roles (Staff, Manager, or Owner) there too — the role controls which sections of the sidebar they can see.
  • 2To clock a staff member in, tap the Clock In icon next to "Owner" at the top of the sidebar. Pick their name from the avatar picker and enter their PIN.
  • 3While an employee is clocked in, their name and role show in the sidebar in place of "Owner." Tap the sign-out icon there to clock them back out and return to full owner access.
  • 4An idle staff session automatically clocks out after the configured inactivity timeout, set in Settings → Staff → Auto Lock After: 15 min, 30 min, 1 hr, or 2 hr.
Tip: No PIN entry is ever required just to open the app as the owner. If you don't see the roster when clocking staff in, add employees first under Labor → Staff.
14

Settings

Settings covers your business profile, tax configuration, currency, payment processor, promo codes, and staff auto-lock. Changes take effect immediately and persist across app restarts.

  • 1Tax Region: Under Tax Configuration, tap Change next to Tax Region. Pick your country and then your province, state, or region. The correct rate is applied instantly. You can still override it manually with the toggle below.
  • 2Business Logo: Under Business Profile, paste a URL to your truck logo image (JPEG or PNG). The logo appears on receipts and the sidebar header. You can also edit your truck description and receipt footer message here.
  • 3Currency: Tap your currency (CAD, USD, INR, PKR) in the Currency section. All prices and reports switch instantly.
  • 4Promo Codes: Tap Add Code in the Promo Codes section to create discount codes. Set the code text, a percentage or fixed discount, and toggle each code active or inactive at any time.
  • 5Language: Tap your preferred language in the Language section — English, Español, Français, हिन्दी, or اردو. This changes the app's display language on this device only, independent of the iPad's own system language.
Tip: Your tax region setting persists across restarts. You only need to set it once. If your jurisdiction changes rates you can update it here in seconds.
1

Setting Up Your Menu

Build your menu before your first service. Add items with prices, choose categories, and set up modifier groups so customers can customize their orders exactly the way they want.

  • 1Tap Menu in the bottom navigation. Tap the + button to add a new item.
  • 2Enter the item name, price, and choose or create a category. Categories appear as filter tabs on the order screen.
  • 3To add modifiers (like size, toppings, or extras), tap Modifier Groups on the item and create or attach a group. Mark groups as required or optional.
  • 4Toggle Available off for items that are sold out. They become greyed out on the order screen and cannot be added to orders.
  • 5Tap Save to publish the item immediately.
Tip: Modifier groups are shared across items. Create a "Protein choice" group once and attach it to every item that offers it.
2

Starting Your Day

On Android, you start each day from the Reports screen. This records your opening float and officially opens the till for the day.

  • 1Tap Reports in the bottom navigation, then tap Start Day.
  • 2Count your opening cash drawer by denomination. Enter each amount. The total appears at the bottom as your opening float.
  • 3Tap Open Till to confirm. The day is now live and the order screen is ready for the first customer.
  • 4Staff clock in using their individual PINs on the lock screen (see section 13).
Tip: You must start the day before any orders can be placed. The order screen shows a banner prompting you to open the till if you try to take an order before starting.
3

Taking a Customer Order

The order screen is your main selling surface. Tap items to add them, configure modifiers, apply discounts, and send to the kitchen in one smooth flow.

  • 1On the Orders tab, tap a menu item to add it to the current order. Use the category tabs at the top to filter items.
  • 2If the item has required modifiers, a sheet slides up automatically. Make the required selections and tap Add to Order.
  • 3Adjust quantities by tapping the item in the order list. Swipe left to remove an item.
  • 4Tap Promo Code to apply a discount, or Custom Discount for a one-off percentage or fixed amount.
  • 5Tap Charge to proceed to payment.
4

Kitchen Display

Paid orders flow to the Kitchen Display automatically. The cook sees each ticket with its items, modifiers, and order number so nothing gets missed.

  • 1Tap Kitchen in the bottom navigation to view the live ticket queue.
  • 2Tap a ticket to mark individual items as prepared. When all items on a ticket are done, tap Complete to archive it.
  • 3Completed tickets move out of the active queue. The cashier is notified the order is ready.
Tip: Run the Kitchen screen on a second device or tablet mounted in the cooking area. It updates in real time as orders come in.
5

Taking Payment

Accept cash, card, or tap-to-pay. The app tracks the split and calculates change for cash payments automatically.

  • 1On the payment screen, choose Cash, Card, or Split.
  • 2For cash, enter the amount tendered. The app shows the change due instantly.
  • 3For card, follow the prompt to tap or swipe via your connected reader.
  • 4Tap Complete Sale. A receipt is generated and the order is sent to the kitchen (if not already done).
  • 5To send a digital receipt, enter the customer's email or phone number on the receipt screen.
6

SMS Notifications ENT

Enterprise plan customers can send automated SMS order-ready notifications so customers know when to pick up without crowding the window.

  • 1On the payment screen, enter the customer's phone number when prompted. The number is saved to the order.
  • 2When the kitchen marks the order complete, an SMS is sent automatically: "Your order #42 is ready for pickup!"
  • 3To enable or disable SMS notifications, go to Settings → Notifications and toggle Order Ready SMS.
7

Tracking Customers PRO

Build a customer list with order history, lifetime spend, and visit counts so you can recognize regulars and run targeted promos.

  • 1Tap Customers in the sidebar. Tap a customer to view their order history and total spend.
  • 2To link an order to a customer, tap Add Customer during checkout and search by name or phone.
  • 3Customers are added automatically when they provide a phone number or email for a receipt.
8

Daily Reports PRO

The Reports screen shows a live snapshot of today's sales, plus access to historical daily summaries and the Start Day and End of Day controls.

  • 1Tap Reports in the bottom navigation. The summary card shows today's order count, gross revenue, and payment breakdown.
  • 2Scroll down to see a breakdown by category, top items, and an hourly sales chart.
  • 3Tap History to browse past daily reports. Tap any day to see full detail.
  • 4On Pro and Enterprise, the end of day report is emailed to your account address automatically when you close the day.
9

Managing Inventory PRO

Track stock counts for ingredients or menu items. The app deducts from stock as orders are placed and alerts you when you are running low.

  • 1Tap Inventory in the sidebar. Tap Add Item to create a tracked stock entry (e.g., "Burger Patties" with a count of 50).
  • 2Link menu items to inventory entries so each sale automatically deducts from stock.
  • 3Set a low stock threshold. When stock falls below the threshold, a warning badge appears in the sidebar.
  • 4To top up stock, tap the item and tap Adjust Count.
10

Logging Expenses PRO

Record costs like fuel, supplies, and permits so your profit and loss report reflects the true cost of running your truck.

  • 1Tap Expenses in the sidebar. Tap Add Expense.
  • 2Enter a name, amount, date, and category (Supplies, Fuel, Labor, Permits, Other).
  • 3Optionally attach a photo of the receipt by tapping the camera icon.
  • 4Saved expenses appear in the expense list and automatically flow into the Profit and Loss report.
11

Profit and Loss PRO

The Profit and Loss screen combines revenue from orders with your logged expenses to give you a clear picture of net profit for any period.

  • 1Tap Reports → Profit and Loss. Select a date range: today, this week, this month, or custom.
  • 2The screen shows gross revenue, total costs by category, and net profit at the bottom.
  • 3Tap any cost category row to see the individual expense entries that make it up.
12

Closing Your Day

End of Day closes the till, records the closing cash count, and locks in the daily report. It is a multi-step flow you reach from the Reports screen.

  • 1Tap Reports in the bottom navigation, then tap End of Day.
  • 2The summary screen shows today's total orders, gross revenue, and payment breakdown. Review it, then tap Next.
  • 3Count your closing cash drawer by denomination and enter each amount. The app shows expected cash (opening float plus all cash sales) and any variance.
  • 4Tap Close Day to finalize. On Pro and Enterprise a report is emailed to your account address automatically. The next app launch will prompt you to start a new day.
Tip: If you are offline when closing, the end of day data is queued locally and syncs to the backend automatically when connectivity is restored.
12b

Order History

Order History gives you a searchable log of every past order with filters for date range and payment method. You can void orders, view itemized receipts, and share receipt PDFs from here.

  • 1Tap Reports → Order History. Use the Period filter to scope results to today, this week, or a custom range.
  • 2Use the Payment filter to show only cash, card, or split orders.
  • 3Tap any order to see the full itemized receipt with modifiers, discounts, tax, and total.
  • 4To void an order, open it and tap Void Order. Confirm the reason. The order is removed from the daily totals and a void record is logged.
  • 5Tap Share Receipt to send the receipt as a PDF via email, AirDrop, or any share target.
13

Staff and PIN Lock

Each staff member on Android has their own 4-digit PIN. When they enter it, the app clocks them in and shows their name in the header. The screen locks automatically after a configurable inactivity period and each person re-enters their own PIN to clock in for the next shift.

  • 1Tap Labor in the sidebar. Go to the Staff tab. Tap a staff member to assign or update their 4-digit PIN.
  • 2When the app is locked, the PIN entry screen appears. Staff tap their 4-digit PIN to clock in. Owner and manager PINs work as an override on any device.
  • 3To clock out, tap Sign Out on the staff badge in the header. The app returns to the PIN lock screen for the next person.
  • 4To change the auto-lock timeout, go to Settings → Staff and choose: 15 min, 30 min, 1 hr, or 2 hr.
Tip: If no staff PINs have been set up yet, a Continue as Owner button appears on the lock screen so you are never locked out during initial setup.
